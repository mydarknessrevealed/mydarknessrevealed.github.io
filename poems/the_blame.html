The Blame

The darkness creeps in my room again.
I cover the windowpane so the light stays dim.
From the way I act, you might call me insane,
But that’s not it—it’s not the same.

To explain what I feel, I wouldn’t know how,
These emotions are ineffable, they ebb and flow.
Like the sea, the tide rolls in, and the tears overflow,
I lose all track of time as memory’s shadows gnaw at my spine.

That existential question rings in my mind—why, why, oh why?
Yet only echoes answer, as reason whispers, "it's all your fault," leaving me in shivers.

David A. Mishoe
