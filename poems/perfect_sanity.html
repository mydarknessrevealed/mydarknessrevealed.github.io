Perfect Sanity

Mad as a hatter,
Mercury slipping through my fingers—
Liquid silver, toxic, elusive.
Does it matter
If the insanity lingers?

Did you see that?
...Did I?
What happens if we both look at the same time?
No, you're crazy, I'm fine.
I'm always on time, never late—
WHO LOCKED THE FUCKING GATE?!

I’m sorry—
Let me reiterate.
Sigh.
Okay. Now, where were we?
No, no, no, that’s all wrong—
Were you even paying attention?

Oh, by the way—did I forget to mention?
You are only a figment of my imagination.
Yet here we are, dancing on the edge of reason.
Going mad, if only for a season.
If that were true, then you wouldn't see me, and I wouldn't be you.

But am I?
I SAID, WERE YOU EVEN LISTENING?!
Am I?

I apologize. I promised I would keep my composure until this was all over, so let us continue, shall we?
Where were we by the way? I’m sorry. I wasn’t paying attention.

For the sake of argument, let’s say we exist
Somewhere between logic and madness,
Between a whisper and a scream, the ego and the id.
Blink—and I vanish.
Wake—and it was only a dream.
Could it ever be that simple?
Isn’t that all we were wanting? Just a moment of peace.

Yet the walls still breathe when I turn away.
You speak in circles but have nothing to say.
That incessant dripping—taunting.
That tick-tick-tock—haunting.

Did we do this already?
Déjà vu like a record skipping in my head.
The needle digs deep into my skin,
But ink spills where blood should’ve been.
Stanzas tattooed themselves into my veins,
Scrawling words I half remember, half invent.

They say madness is just seeing too much.
Hearing too well.
Feeling too deep.
So tell me my friend—who decides reality?

Your reflection lingers too long in the mirror.
And I wonder:
Who’s the real one standing here?
And as I realize it may not be me,
Something cold curls ‘round my spine—
Fear,
Quiet and absolute, intimidating, but honest.

Mad as a hatter.
Mercury slipped through my fingers.
It always mattered.
And the insanity lingers.

Perfect Sanity (Expanded)

Mad as a hatter, raddled and wan,
Mercury slipping through fingers long gone—
A wyrd tide, ebbing, flowing, ne’er still,
Elusive as phantoms that bend to my will.

Dost thou see it?
…Dost I?
What fate unfurls if our gazes entwine?
No, thou art daft—I am fine.
I keep the hours, steadfast and true—
WHO HATH SEALED THE PORTAL THROUGH?!

Forgive me—
I prithee, let me speak anew.
Sigh.
Now, where wert we?
Nay, nay—this is not right—
Didst thou even listen?

By my troth—did I forget to mention?
Thou art but a specter, a figment most fey.
Yet here we prance, on ruin’s edge,
Flitting ‘twixt reason and the abyssal fray.

And if thou be phantom—then what of me?
An echo? A shade? A hollow decree?
Or but a reflection that lingers too long,
Some fractured hymn of an unwritten song?

DIDST THOU HEAR ME?!
Am I?

I beg pardon. I swore to keep poise—
To clasp my mind in an iron vise
Until this wretched page be writ—
So shall we press on?
Forgive me—I was not paying heed.

Let us, for argument’s sake, presume we are—
Somewhere betwixt logic and lunacy,
Wavering on the keenest blade,
Caught ‘twixt whisper and wail,
Ego and id,
The silver sliver of an eye half-lidded.

Blink—and I fade.
Wake—and I was naught but a dream.

Could it be thus?
Is this all we ever sought?
A quiet moment of stillness,
Where even the walls might cease their breath?

Yet they do not.
They pulse.
They chime.
They creep.

They know.

The ceaseless drip—
The thrice-cursed tock—
The cadence of something fey and foul,
Clawing, clutching, waiting.

Have we lived this ere?
A quirk of fate, a cycle unbroken,
A sorcery most fell?
Deja vu—like a needle cleaving flesh,
Sinking deep—
But ink flows where blood should seep.

Stanzas have burned themselves upon my veins,
Each line a tethered memory, half-formed,
Half-true.

They say madness is but knowing too much,
Hearing too keen,
Feeling too deep.

So tell me, my wight—who deigns to shape reality?

Thy likeness lingers o’long in the mirror,
A smirking jest, a silent taunt.
I would ask thee—who stands before me now?
Yet I fear, most utterly,
That thou wouldst ask the same.

And as this thought takes root and coils,
A breath of frost doth line my spine,
A creeping hand, insidious and sure—
Dread.

Quiet and absolute.
Ancient and hoar.

Mad as a hatter, raddled and wan,
Mercury slipping through fingers long gone.
It mattered.
It always mattered.
And still—the insanity lingers.

David A. Mishoe
